Types of Carnation

Standard Carnations

Standard carnations are the most common type of carnation and are known for their large, full blooms. They come in a wide range of colors, including red, white, pink, and yellow. Standard carnations are often used in bouquets and floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.

Miniature or Dwarf Carnations

Miniature carnations are smaller in size compared to standard carnations and are often used as fillers in floral arrangements. They come in a variety of colors and have a delicate, dainty appearance. Miniature carnations are versatile and can be used in a wide range of floral designs.

Spray Carnations

Spray carnations, also known as cluster carnations, have multiple blooms on each stem, giving them a fuller and more voluminous appearance. They are available in a variety of colors and are often used in bouquets and corsages. Spray carnations add texture and dimension to floral arrangements.

Different carnations hold various symbolic meanings, often based on their color:

Love and Affection: Carnations, in general, symbolize love, fascination, and affection. They can be given to express deep emotions, whether romantic or platonic.

Mother's Love:Light red or pink carnations are commonly associated with maternal love and are often given on Mother's Day to honor and appreciate mothers.

Admiration:Dark red carnations convey deep admiration and respect for someone. They can be given to show appreciation for someone's accomplishments or qualities.

Purity and Good Luck:White carnations symbolize purity, innocence, and good luck. They are often used in weddings and religious ceremonies.

Loyalty:Striped or variegated carnations can represent a sense of fascination and distinction, conveying admiration or loyalty.

Regret or Remembrance:In some contexts, purple carnations are associated with regret or longing. They can also symbolize remembrance of someone who has passed away.

New Beginnings:Green carnations are sometimes linked to new beginnings and can represent a fresh start or renewal.

Symbol of Socialism:In some cultures, red carnations have been associated with socialism and workers' rights movements.
